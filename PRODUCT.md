# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A couple managing shared household finances together. Not financial professionals — two people who want to know where their money goes without it feeling like a chore. They use this on their phones, probably at the kitchen table or on the sofa, in brief sessions when they've just paid a bill or received a paycheck. Each person has their own financial footprint within the shared household; they need to see both the individual and the joint picture at a glance.

## Product Purpose

Budge is a shared household budget tracker for two. It lets a couple record income, expenses, savings, and debts split across two owners and a joint pot. Firebase keeps both people in sync. Success looks like: at any moment, either person can open the app, see where the household stands, add a transaction in under ten seconds, and feel less anxious about money — not more.

## Brand Personality

Calm, domestic, personal. The voice of a well-kept shared notebook, not a fintech dashboard. Warm but not twee. Confident without being authoritative.

## Anti-references

- No gamification: no streaks, badges, XP, or reward loops. Progress is its own reward.
- Not a generic productivity app: no Notion-style infinite canvas, no task-management metaphors, no "workspace" framing. This is a household tool, not a workspace.

## Design Principles

1. **Personal over professional** — This was built for two specific people, not for a market. Design decisions should feel like they were made by someone who uses the app daily, not engineered for scale or investor demos.
2. **The numbers serve the people** — Data appears when it's useful and steps back when it isn't. No metric-dense layouts, no dashboard-for-its-own-sake. Every chart earns its screen time.
3. **Shared without friction** — Owner attribution (Niamh / Rupert / Joint) is the product's defining structural idea. It should feel as natural as saying "that's yours, this is mine, and here's ours" — not like a permission system.
4. **Calm as the default register** — Financial anxiety is real. Nothing should flash, pulse, badge, or urgently demand attention. The interface is a quiet place to get clear on money.
5. **Domestic familiarity** — Reach for the language and feel of a shared household first. Bank language and fintech conventions are a last resort, not a default.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best effort: fix obvious issues (missing labels, broken keyboard navigation, low-contrast text on coloured backgrounds). No formal WCAG level target, but the two-person owner palette (coral, blue, green) must maintain readable contrast against both light and dark surface tokens.
